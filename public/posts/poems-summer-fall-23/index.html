Collection of poems written between Summer and Fall 2023.

T he poems below don’t claim all the answers; these poems are less of a proclamation and more of an exploration. They stem from personal observations and experiences, encapsulating moments and passages of my life that have weighed heavy on my mind. Any pretense or theatrics latent in the writing is unintentional. The text is simply a reflection of my own thoughts and feelings at a snapshot in time. Much of the content here originated from a manic-depressive diagnosis in Fall ‘22, and subsequent medication and episodes.

My hope is that in preserving these deeply personal pieces here, I will provide myself a place to engage with this snapshot of my mind for future exploration and hopefully maturation - without judgement. After leaving my writing behind for some time, I often come back and cringe unfairly at my former self. I consider this an exercise in patience and understanding with regard to prior states of mind, and an effort to not leave those lessons behind.

1. The Third Alternative

1

Standing alone in the darkened kitchen
Staring inside the refrigerator
Awash in the artificial glow

Inside, stacks of bread we turned from stone †1
Hungry - but not appetized
Closed, back to bed

In sleep, dreams of the past
Shuffling off the bus into an empty house
To wait after dark for the low warm rumble
Of the garage door

Three then two
They chose the third alternative
Left a life behind
Little light to follow

Two then one,
Waking to shoulder new pressures
To work, sleep, repeat
Following in muddy footsteps

We choose the third alternative
And raise a banner against the others
“Let us feed ourselves, and then ask of us virtue.” †2

The next night awash in light again
Still unsatisfied
Back to bed.

2. This Time Last Year

This time last year
I was pulled and driven.
This time last year
I was digging an empty vein.

Soon it ran dry,
I was red in the joints and nerves.
I was fine for a while
Enjoying the softest shoulder of the year.

Now chased by the urge
To always remain in motion,
Legs shaking the whole way,
Trying not to look down.

When the ligatures eased
I sank in myself
And stayed there for months
Only to come out with the leaves.

This year I’m optimistic
Here at the door of Autumn,
A season often so high
Now braced against it.

3. Judgement in the Water

Good job, friend
Of making a fool of yourself
Look at what you are
And what you thought you ought to be

This is your wake-up call
Your judgement is reflected
Can’t handle it, can you?
What a hypocrite.

Is it weakness or a label?
Is it virtue or is it sin?
Your opinion is inverted
What a hypocrite.

You can change your definition
Alter your harsh perspective
When the finger is pointed at you
Isn’t it so much easier

Though you are a hypocrite
You are still learning
What it means to be labeled
And what it means to judge

Keep learning, hypocrite
You’ll make the same mistake
But at least you now know
Where you went wrong

4. August in Motion

Joints ground to dust
Screeching sonic cones
Rusty teeth bite skin
Steel discs warble

After the session
Dive into numbers
Obsessive recording
In figures and notes

Adreneline flood
Tubes glow red hot
Sleep only a memory
Veering to collapse

Diagnosis hit hard
Forced to rest yet harder
Set an alarm
For April 31

5. These Legs Ache to Move

The demon hit my capillaries
And latched on my nerves
Silenced the rush
But grew under my skin

Twenty, fourty, sixty, done
They say this works well
Maybe it does, but I’m not so sure
Its worth what I feel

My legs ache to move
In the shed in the Fall
At the winter retreat
Bed, desk, or in motion

The panic grows steadily
Till I cant stand anymore
Take me out of the ring
Give me something else

6. Bed and Desk

Dreaming on my haunches in the green room
Soaking in winter dust shelled from the heater
What is anything to muster in the cold
When the lens is blurred

Maybe get by with a short placid stare
Grateful for evergreens under the gray bloom
Stuck in their sap for the rest of the season
Balled up and vacant in blue sheets

Anchored by gravity on my dark perch
Waiting in sedentary shallow heartbeats
Outside, the snowflake spectre hangs in the gloom
Keep me in, warm, away from the rasp

Waking from antidotes by the dose sum
Deaf to the swell in the background coming in turn
To answer the damage of motion in August
The crescendo not a moment too soon

7. No Sleep / Empty House

How did I only sleep from 3 A.M.
Catching sleep in the window of dawn
Barely able to wake, shower, dress
And get out the door

Walking in the cold
Past houses three six and nine
Hair freezing from the tips
Rousing in footsteps

Fire and blast beats scorch my little wires
All the way to the bus stop and to school
Always sitting alone
Living in my own head

Shuffle into school
Up the bleachers in the gym
Waiting to dispatch to class
At the first bell

Single-strap backpack and binders of loose papers
Scribbles and drafts, incoherent and incomplete
Now barely awake, unfed, and unhappy
To the first bed-of-desk

There I sleep from class to class
Snoozing between alarm bells
Fists balled up in the sleeves
Of my dirty green zip-up hoodie

My neck aches from the posture
The teachers given up on waking me
Lapsing and coming to
Dreams and lecture inverted

Lunch alone most of the time
Or on the edge of the conversation
Blackberry soda and frozen food
Made me sick more than once

Last bell rings; we rush to the gym
To wait on the school bus home
Sweethearts grope and kiss in their body spray
boys scratch obscenities in the bleachers

Back on the bus, dropped off near home
Only to shuffle into an empty house
Parents away, at the office, on an airplane
Silence, my day begins

Sitting down at the console and television
I waste the afternoon away on video games and junk food
Night falls early, someone comes home,
Though I don’t check who

The clock passess midnight but I’m still awake
I never can sleep at night; no one tells me why
Screens paint my reality, I dream in the day
The classroom my bedroom will remain.
